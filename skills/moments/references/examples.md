## How To Use These Cases

Pick the closest case, then adapt the structure to the user's actual project profile and evidence. If a fact is missing, weaken the claim instead of filling it in.

### Case 1: Product Material With Weak Evidence

Input: a service description and scattered selling points, but no customer proof.

Use:
- Main type: 产品.
- Four-layer focus: 观点层 = why this problem matters now; 框架层 = scene → friction → method → fit.
- Output should explain fit and next step.

Avoid:
- Do not claim "很多客户反馈" or "转化提升" without evidence.
- Do not turn every feature into a slogan.

### Case 2: Case Material With Real Feedback

Input: anonymized user feedback, before/after notes, and a process timeline.

Use:
- Main type: 案例.
- Structure: background困境 → key action → visible change → reflection.
- Keep direct evidence in `material-analysis.md` and privacy notes in `quality-review.md`.

Avoid:
- Do not expose names, order IDs, chat handles, or sensitive business data.
- Do not inflate a single user's result into a universal promise.

### Case 3: Launch Material

Input: enrollment date, target audience, offer details, quota, and a founder note.

Use:
- Main type: 发售.
- Structure: belief/value → who it helps → what is included → why now → soft action.
- Scarcity details must match the source material exactly.

Avoid:
- Do not invent deadlines, bonuses, discounts, or remaining spots.
- Do not use forced engagement such as reposting or comment-gating.

### Case 4: Personal Brand Daily Note

Input: a short daily observation, a photo idea, and project positioning.

Use:
- Main type: 人设 or 生活.
- Keep sentence rhythm looser than a sales post.
- One grounded detail is better than three abstract lessons.

Avoid:
- Do not force a product pitch if the material is meant to build trust.
- Do not end with generic inspirational summary.
